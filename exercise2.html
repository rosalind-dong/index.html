Overview

This page expands Exercise 1 by focusing on structural HTML: <header>, <nav>, <main>, <section>, <article>, <aside>, and <footer>.

The goal is to make the page readable not only visually, but also structurally (for browsers, screen readers, and future maintainers).

Reading Notes (example content)

Article

An <article> is a self-contained piece of content that could stand alone (like a blog post, news item, or entry).

  • Clear heading
  • Independent meaning
  • Reusable outside the page

Aside

An <aside> holds related but non-essential material—like a sidebar, definition, or quick context note.

Tip: Using aside makes your layout and meaning clearer than using only div.
